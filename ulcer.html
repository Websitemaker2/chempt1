Apparatus and Materials

• Glasses - we use them as a container of water with different temperatures. • Kettle: an instrument to have boiling water • Pitcher - a container of cold water • Bowls - containers of the two different colored powders • Spoons - we use them to transfer the powders from bowls to glasses. • Milk powder and coffee powder - these two are the powders with different colors that we used in our experiment. • Water - this is the element we use in our experiment to observe and hypothesize the possible cause of why certain solids dissolve faster in liquids with a higher temperature than others.

Procedure

• We find a spot to conduct our experiment. • We get all the equipment we need for the experiment. - Glasses - Kettle - Pitcher - Bowls - Spoons • We get all the needed materials for the experiment. - Milk - Coffee - Water • We prepared the hot and cold water - Boil a cup of water in the kettle for 3 minutes. - Get the stored pitcher with cold water in the refrigerator. • Given that we had prepared all the needed equipment and materials, we began to conduct our experiment. - We place two transparent glasses on the table. - Put the hot water in one glass and the cold water in the other glass. - Put the coffee in the hot water. - Put the milk in the cold water. Given that, we wait for the results.

Safety Precautions

When dealing with hot water or the kettle, we recommend using potholders to avoid burns and always keeping children away to prevent accidents.
